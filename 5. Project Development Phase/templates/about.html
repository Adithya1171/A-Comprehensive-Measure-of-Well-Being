WHAT IS THE HUMAN DEVELOPMENT INDEX?

The **Human Development Index (HDI)** is a summary composite measure introduced by the United Nations Development Programme (UNDP) in 1990. It was created to emphasize that people and their capabilities should be the ultimate criteria for assessing the development of a country, rather than economic growth alone.

The HDI ranges from **0.000 to 1.000** and represents the geometric mean of normalized indices for three key dimensions of human development:

  • Long and healthy life: Assessed by life expectancy at birth.
  • Knowledge: Assessed by expected years of schooling for children and mean years of schooling for adults.
  • Decent standard of living: Assessed by Gross National Income (GNI) per capita (PPP$).

MACHINE LEARNING WORKFLOW

This project represents a complete, end-to-end data science and machine learning pipeline integrated into a web interface:

1. Data Cleaning & Preprocessing

Duplicates removed, missing variables interpolated, and features standardized.

2. Feature Scaling

`StandardScaler` applied to prevent skewness from GNI scale differences.

3. Model Training

A Linear Regression model is trained using an 80/20 train-test split.

4. Flask Integration & DB logs

The serialized model predicts HDI scores on forms input and saves results in SQLite.
